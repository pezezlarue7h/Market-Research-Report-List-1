<p><ul><li>Upstream Biomanufacturing</li><li>Downstream Biomanufacturing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Next-Generation Biomanufacturing encompasses two main market types: upstream and downstream biomanufacturing. Upstream biomanufacturing involves the initial stages of production, including cell line development, fermentation, and cell culture processes, where raw materials are transformed into biological products. In contrast, downstream biomanufacturing focuses on the purification and formulation of these products, including separation, filtration, and quality control measures to ensure the final product meets regulatory standards. Together, these processes enable efficient production of biopharmaceuticals and other biologically-derived materials.</p></p>
