<p><p>The plant-based leather market features several types, including sugar cane, pineapple, cork, and others. Sugar cane leather utilizes the fibrous residue from sugar production, offering a sustainable alternative. Pineapple leather, derived from the leaves of pineapple plants, provides durability and a unique texture. Cork leather, extracted from the bark of cork oak trees, is waterproof and lightweight. Other materials may include rubber, apple waste, and mushroom leather, all contributing to a diverse, eco-friendly market focused on reducing animal-based leather dependency.</p></p>
