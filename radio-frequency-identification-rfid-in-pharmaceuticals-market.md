Chipped RFID uses tags embedded with microchips, enabling accurate tracking and authentication of products throughout the supply chain. Chipless RFID, on the other hand, relies on inexpensive, no-battery tags that can be printed directly on packaging, offering a cost-effective solution for tracking. Both types contribute to reducing counterfeit drugs, ensuring compliance, and streamlining operations in the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
